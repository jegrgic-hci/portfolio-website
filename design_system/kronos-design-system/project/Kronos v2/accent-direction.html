Brand decision · Voice + Color

Pick a personality first, then we build depth around it.

Your wireframe blue and the current Kronos teal are analogous hues — they live in the same family. Picking between them isn't a depth question; it's a voice question. Lab teal is muted, clinical, reserved. Cerulean engineer is saturated, confident, conversational. Both can carry a full 11-step scale (shown below). The right move is to commit to one and let everything else — voice, copy, even the section codes — follow.

Path A · Current direction Lab teal

Voice: clinical, brief, mechanical. The brand is a piece of equipment running a protocol. Colors stay muted on purpose — saturation would feel marketing-y. Pairs with all-caps mono chrome, version stamps, decimal section codes.

Honest take

These are not "depth" alternatives to each other — they're different brands. Both have equivalent depth potential once you build out the 11-step scale (shown above). The decision is which personality you want to ship.

The cerulean is a stronger fit for the construction product specifically. Construction project managers spend their day in tools that look like this — Procore, Autodesk Construction Cloud, Bluebeam. Your wireframe instinct is right for that context. But it will pull the brand voice away from "lab/protocol" toward "engineering software," which means rewriting the copywriting style and probably softening the all-caps chrome.

The lab teal is the stronger fit if Kronos is your portfolio brand first and the construction product is one application of it. Identity stays distinctive; the construction product can use a complement (ochre, clay) for its product-specific moments without losing the parent brand.
