Designer review · 17 May 2026 · Kronos v2 → v2.5

v2 is a solid foundation. It's also a little quiet.

The accessibility fixes landed and the component library is comprehensive. But sitting with it as a product designer, six things stand out as unrealized potential — opportunities to make Kronos feel distinctive in a product context, not just correct. Below are six modernization levers ranked by impact and brand risk. Pick the ones that fit your appetite.

What v2 already gets right — don't touch these.

Keep

Hard 1px edges. The whole identity rests on this; softening it kills the brand.

Keep

Two-register casing (mono uppercase chrome, sentence body). Distinctive and now AA-safe.

Keep

Teal as the only chromatic accent. Resist adding a second brand color.

Keep

Lab/protocol voice and version stamps. Charmingly specific. Most systems are voiceless.

01

Make the display face do real work

Low risk · High impact

Anton only appears in the wordmark. That's the brand's most distinctive piece of type and it's relegated to a single moment. In a product UI, large numerals — KPI metrics, dates, counts, section codes — are the natural place for display type to shine. Right now those moments are set in IBM Plex Mono, which is fine, but it's the same face as every label and button. Display type is wasted as decoration.

02

Expand the neutral scale (and pick a temperature)

Low risk · Med impact

v2 has four greys (fg-1 through fg-4) plus three borders. That's enough for a portfolio. For a product surface with stacked cards, tables, side panels, and modals, it isn't — designers end up using border-light against light-grey-on-white and everything blurs. Modern systems use an 11-step neutral ladder. Also: pure greys read clinical-cold. A 1° cool tint reads modern and engineered; a 1° warm tint reads humane. Either is fine — pure neutral is the unconfident choice.

03

Allow elevation — but only on floating UI

Med risk · Med impact

v2's "no shadows" rule was built for the portfolio. In a product with stacked layers (modal over page, dropdown over content, toast over everything), flat 1px borders blur the depth and become a usability problem — users can't see what's on top. Modern systems keep flat-by-default but reserve one or two elevation tokens for floating things only. Cards stay flat. Dropdowns/modals/toasts/popovers lift.

04

Let teal be loud — as a surface, not just a stroke

Low risk · High impact

Right now teal whispers. It's a 2px sidebar rail, a tiny border on the "Featured" tag, an underline on a hovered link. Brand colors do more brand work when they occasionally take over a surface — hero promos, empty states, marketing callouts. The trick is reserving it for one moment per page so it stays earned, not decorative.

05

Two density modes — cozy & compact

Med risk · Med impact

v2 is calibrated for portfolio scale: generous padding, 40px inputs, 24px gaps. That breaks for data-heavy product UIs — admin tables, planner views, log readers — where users need to see 20 rows on a screen, not 7. Most modern systems ship a density mode toggle (or per-component size prop) and define both scales as tokens.

06

Name the motion — not just the durations

Low risk · Low impact

v2 has two timing tokens (--k2-t-fast: 120ms and --k2-t-med: 150ms) and one easing. That covers hovers, but real product UIs have entrance moments — a modal sliding in, a toast announcing itself, a tab indicator gliding to its new spot. Without named tokens, these get hand-rolled per feature and drift. Ship a small motion vocabulary.

quick: hover/press color changes. base: small state shifts (focus ring, tab indicator). emphasis: a real moment (button success state). enter: a thing appearing (modal, toast, drawer). All collapse to 0ms under prefers-reduced-motion.

Verdict Cheap to add, pays off when you build the dashboard. Most useful is --motion-enter — modal/drawer/toast all need a calmer 300ms-ish entrance than v2 currently allows.

My recommendation — three tiers

Ship this week

The "free wins" tier

  • 01 · Anton on metrics, section codes, dashboard hero numerals.
  • 04 · Allow one teal surface moment per page.
  • 06 · Add the four motion tokens.

Low brand risk, immediate visual upgrade. All three are token-level or new specimens, no breaking changes.

Ship next sprint

The "for product use" tier

  • 02 · Expand neutrals to 11 steps with a cool 1° tint.
  • 03 · Add two elevation tokens, gate to floating UI only.
  • 05 · Add density tokens for tables and lists.

These pay off when you build the construction-engineering dashboard. Worth a focused sprint before that work starts.

Don't ship yet

The "wait and see" tier

  • Full dark mode — needs the neutral expansion (02) first.
  • Data viz palette — wait until a real chart need shows up.
  • Iconography expansion — only when the hand-drawn set starts breaking.

Resist building these on spec. Add them when a real product surface needs them — that way the decisions are grounded, not speculative.
