02 Research goals and Objective
Research and data collection methods
Shopify rolling research program |
There is a round of rolling research that will be conducted in mid April that we will be submitting to. Unfortunately, we are not guaranteed a spot given the limited number of research topics allocated.
However, if our research topic were selected, we would be able to run some concept and high level usability tests with up to 6 sellers to get feedback on the content, designs, and overall impression of the feature.
Rough methodology 6 participants 20 min sessions Intro/background question about CRM/Email capture 1-click flow concept/usability test Settings flow concept/usability test Overall feedback
Alpha launch survey
During the Alpha Launch of this feature in April 2023, there is the opportunity to include a brief survey to sellers selected to get feedback on perceived ease of use and satisfaction.
Two questions can be used to calculate a Single Usability Metric (SUM) which has high measurement validity and correlation to the SUS measurement. This data, along with descriptive usage metrics, can be used to infer the performance of the feature based on our research goals and objectives.
Further, we can ask these sellers if we they would be interested in participating in UXR. This would help with recruitment hurdles and expedite the research process while building our depth of knowledge in how ensure this feature is adopted by sellers
Rough methodology Two questions at end of Alpha Launch for selected sellers One question about desire to participate in future research.
UXR usability test and interview
Either stand alone, or combined with the Alpha Launch survey, we can do a moderated usability test (both qual and quant metrics) as well as ask deeper questions surrounding email capture to fully understand sellers goals, practices, and how our feature compares to what they currently do or are wanting to do.
This option has the draw back of likely taking the most time for recruitment and execution, but should provide the breadth and depth of research about this feature for future iterations.
