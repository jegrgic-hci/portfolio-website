Findings
Key Finding: While most participants easily understood how to enable the SFI email capture feature, they lacked confidence due to the absence of a settings confirmation or preview screen. Sellers were hesitant to enable the feature without the ability to customize or see the customer experience beforehand.
Key Insights:
Sellers need customization options for discount messaging and functionality to align with their brand and legal requirements.
A preview screen and multi-step enablement process would build seller confidence and improve adoption rates.
The current "Turn On" link lacks clarity; sellers expect a toggle or more intuitive UI.
Recommendations
Introduce a multi-step flow with settings and a preview before enabling the feature.
Provide customization controls for discount amounts, messaging, and branding.
Redesign the enablement interaction (e.g., replace "Turn On" with a toggle).

Context
Within SMA, there is no method for sellers to enable and config Seller Funded Incentives (SFI) due to contractual and technical blockers of email capture between Shopify and SMA. However, as of February 2023, an agreement has been reached to allow the set-up and config of SFI email capture within SMA. This new feature is expected increase seller adoption of incentives, improving Meta's price parity situation, and drive 0.63% incremental Shops Ads GMV
Project objective
To help sellers become aware and adopt email opt-in with an incentive within SMA. This will help sellers to collect more customer email addresses so they can re-engage with them and drive LTV.
Goal
Business Goal: Drive adoption of 1-click email capture SFI to drive promotional parity and protect Shops Ads performance as we reduce reliance on MFI (H1 goal: drive adoption with 1700 TAM sellers which represent 20% of Shops ads revenue, H2 goal is to increase adoption among sellers which represent 50% of Shops Ads revenue)
Research Goals: Understand key pathways for adoption, which surfaces should we prioritize to drive adoption? (SMA, email, Ads Manager, etc) Ensure that end to end flow is frictionless and sets clear expectations with sellers so they know what they're opting in to Understand which unmet needs are most important to sellers to help us with prioritization

03 Findings

Key Findings

Understanding & Usability

Most participants understood how to enable the feature via the "Turn On" link but hesitated to proceed due to lack of confidence. Sellers expected to be directed to a settings page or pop-up upon enabling where they could confirm, customize, and preview the customer experience.

Customization Needs

Sellers expressed strong desire for customization of discount messaging to align with brand strategy and legal policies. Many sellers felt the feature lacked flexibility for adjusting discount amounts or modifying opt-in messaging before enabling it.

Content Clarity

While the feature description on the SMA home page was generally clear, some sellers were unsure about where the discount originated or how it would apply (checkout vs. storefront). Misunderstandings arose about whether the discount was linked to Shopify or third-party platforms like Klaviyo.

Expectations for Control

Sellers expected more control over the activation process, including options for modifying discount amounts, customizing email messaging, and previewing the customer experience. Many expected a toggle or clearer UI elements (rather than a "Turn On" link) to enable/disable the feature.

Concerns with One-Click Enablement

The 1-click enablement process was seen as too rigid, which discouraged adoption. Sellers wanted a multi-step process for greater transparency and control.

04 Next steps
04 Next Steps
Rethink 1-click option
All participants understood the feature, however, sellers expected to be able to configure and customize the way email collection would appear on their shop surfaces. Therefore, to facilitate Seller adoption, it may be more beneficial to create a multistep enablement process that gives sellers control and feedback on discount amount, location, language, and design/branding.
Alpha Launch Survey
The survey that is part of the Alpha Launch of the survey and includes a question about how well the feature meets sellers needs. If this result is also low, it provides more evidence that sellers want more control and/or feedback on how the feature will be enabled on their shop.

Research and data collection methods
Shopify rolling research program |
There is a round of rolling research that will be conducted in mid April that we will be submitting to. Unfortunately, we are not guaranteed a spot given the limited number of research topics allocated.
However, if our research topic were selected, we would be able to run some concept and high level usability tests with up to 6 sellers to get feedback on the content, designs, and overall impression of the feature.
Rough methodology 6 participants 20 min sessions Intro/background question about CRM/Email capture 1-click flow concept/usability test Settings flow concept/usability test Overall feedback
Alpha launch survey
During the Alpha Launch of this feature in April 2023, there is the opportunity to include a brief survey to sellers selected to get feedback on perceived ease of use and satisfaction.
Two questions can be used to calculate a Single Usability Metric (SUM) which has high measurement validity and correlation to the SUS measurement. This data, along with descriptive usage metrics, can be used to infer the performance of the feature based on our research goals and objectives.
Further, we can ask these sellers if we they would be interested in participating in UXR. This would help with recruitment hurdles and expedite the research process while building our depth of knowledge in how ensure this feature is adopted by sellers
Rough methodology Two questions at end of Alpha Launch for selected sellers One question about desire to participate in future research.
UXR usability test and interview
Either stand alone, or combined with the Alpha Launch survey, we can do a moderated usability test (both qual and quant metrics) as well as ask deeper questions surrounding email capture to fully understand sellers goals, practices, and how our feature compares to what they currently do or are wanting to do.
This option has the draw back of likely taking the most time for recruitment and execution, but should provide the breadth and depth of research about this feature for future iterations.
02 Research goals and Objective
Research and data collection goals
Shopify rolling research program |
Behavioral metrics provide a valuable insight into the performance of a feature, but only answers ‘what’ users are doing. Compared to predefined success metrics, this may be sufficient data collection.
However, for a new feature, without existing benchmark metrics to judge success, it is important to perform some qualitative research to understand ‘why’ users are behaving in a particular way.
Both forms of data collection and research help develop a fuller and detailed understanding of how users interact with the feature and how it can be improved to meet their needs.
