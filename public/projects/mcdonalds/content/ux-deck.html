Mobile Payment, Loyalty, & Post Fulfillment CX

Tasked with creating a contactless mobile payment system within the existing application. This involved mapping overlap between mobile ordering and expected contactless payment functionality, then incorporating additional capabilities to improve customer experience and align with business objectives.

Design Principles

Mobile Payment and Loyalty

01
A Single Check-in Per Visit
  • Use GMA only once to redeem a Deal, Pay, and Earn Points — fewer eCP calls
  • No GMA-side aggregation necessary; Pay badge easily added to Deals rather than requiring two separate flows
  • Clean design doesn't add another sidebar item; payment is a ubiquitous element
02
Unambiguous for Crew, Secure for Customers
  • Crew knows if check-in occurred but sees no unnecessary account details
  • Customer selects payment method in GMA, consistent with the Deal flow
  • Consistent with customer's Mobile Ordering experience and expectations
  • Payments are tokenized, PII is avoided, app can incorporate biometric lock
03
Add Surprise and Delight
  • Rewards don't need to be selected to be redeemed — an opportunity for delight
  • Payment and Loyalty check-in are relatively hands-free
  • A single check-in minimizes friction and wait time for customers
  • Increase feedback by making the Voice Survey more accessible
04
Speed Up the Drive Thru
  • Alpha-code check-in and single check-in minimize the payment step
  • Post-fulfillment Help speeds up DT by confirming order in pull-forward

System Design

Business Model Evolution

Had to redefine the business model for what type of customer information is passed to a location based on when and where a customer is. This also required determining accessibility of information and specific triggers to comply with legal requirements.

Mobile Order — Declared Intent
Customer places mobile order designating pick-up location and payment method.
Order sent to cloud until customer arrives at location.
Customer crosses geofence — order pushed from cloud to location.
Customer appears on POS with full order details. Crew member finalizes order.
On-Site Order — No Declared Intent
Customer arrives on-site. No prior order declared in app.
Customer crosses geofence and accesses payment badge. Unique code or QR generated from intent (payment, gift card, deal).
Customer appears on POS with name only for soft check-in — enables recents, favorites, and loyalty earning.
Customer provides code or QR to crew for hard check-in, enabling saved payment for order.
Feature Design

Badge Access

Introducing a new feature within the app created a need to provide both an easy persistent access method and explicit contextual entry points throughout to drive adoption.

Burger Menu
A persistent "Pay & Earn" button within the navigation menu gives customers a reliable access point from anywhere in the app.
Shake to Access
Users can shake their phone to access the badge from anywhere in the application — a gesture-based shortcut surfaced through the onsite discovery moment.
My Rewards
Within 'My Rewards', a CTA allows customers to earn loyalty points by presenting their badge — reinforcing the value of check-in at the point of motivation.
Deals
Within 'Deals', each available deal surfaces a CTA to redeem it with the badge — tying payment and loyalty check-in directly to deal redemption intent.
Onboarding Moment

Access — Shake Discovery

In order to have users discover they can shake the app to access the badge, a specific 'home' screen is triggered by the geofence crossing — encouraging users to discover the payment and loyalty badge via shaking.

Shake to Earn Points!
01
When the customer first opens the app on-site, coins fall from the top of the application and bounce around using the phone's physics engine.
02
The falling coins encourage customers to shake their phone to move them — naturally discovering the shake-to-access gesture.
03
This moment is triggered specifically by the geofence crossing, so it's contextually relevant — the customer is on-site and the badge is immediately useful.
04
"Surprise and delight" principle in practice — a playful, memorable interaction that teaches the gesture without instruction copy.
